OVERVIEW: The life cycle assessment(LCA) of onesie is conducted in this report. Feasible recommendations are put forwards based on the LCA research output. In this report, two types of baby onesie was considered: single-used and reused. The manufacture, transportation, and use stage in China and United Kingdom is compared. The feasibility of the recommendations is assessed through the LCA output.

INTRODUCTION OF ONESIS

WHAT IS ONESIE

Single-use Onesie

COTTON BLEND WITH POLYESTER, LINEN, AND SYNTHETIC MATERIAL

Reusable Onesie

COTTON CONTENT > 90%

ONESIE: Baby onesies are a common item of clothing for new-born babies because they are safe and comfortable. There are two type of onesis on the market: single-use onesie and reusable onesie. People with different consumption habits will choose different types of onesies. Some parents will worry about microorganism breeding of using one onesie for a long time, they choose the single-use onesie to avoid the troublesome washing process. The others believe the full cotton reusable onesie is comfortable for their babies to wear.

LIFE CYCLE ASSESSMENT

BOUNDARY

THE AIM OF THIS STUDY: The objective of the LCA study is to assess the eco-credentials and perform a life cycle analysis of materials used in the production of onesie for the retail industry to reduce the impact of products in the environment. The reported result could be used to aid policymakers to factor in sustainability considerations when drafting contingency plans for a future retail industry. It can also help to guide public messaging. In this LCA study, the entire value chain from material acquisition to the end of life is considered as shown in Fig. 1. Adhering to the recommendation, Baby onesies (for 0~6 months) are contaminated once worn and should not be reused or recycled. Despite the availability of baby onesie recycling facilities such as those reported in, there are no known facilities in China or United Kingdom that process used baby onesie. Thus, recycling of baby onesie will not be considered in this study. A disposed baby onesie will be treated as municipal waste and thus will be incinerated in a waste-to-energy plant before ending up in the landfill. Summary of findings. LIFE CYCLE ASSESSMENT SUMMARY

Relative Contribution to Each Impact Category for Onesie

GLOBAL WARMING POTENTIAL (GWP): Global warming potential (GWP): the largest portion of GWP in the life cycle of onesie is related to the dying process(50%), a large amount CO2 is emitted when coal is burned to generate steam in the dying process. The making-up stage is the second contributor to GWP for large electricity consumption in this stage[2-6].

.

WATER USAGE (WU): Cotton cultivation contributes nearly 60% of water usage in the life cycle of onesie, followed by the use stage (10%) and dying process(7%)[3].

.

HUMAN TOXICITY POTENTIAL(HTP): The cotton cultivation, dyeing, making-up, and use stages contribute 27.9, 18.1, 26.9, and 14.6 % to the total of HTP, respectively. The source of toxicity is the dyes and auxiliaries used in the dyeing process, detergent in the use stage, the CO2 and NOx emitted during the coal burning, and the heavy metal from fertilizer in the cotton cultivation stage[4].

.

RECOMMENDATION

HOW CAN WE DO BETTER?

IRRIGATION

Take more water-saving irrigation technique and use safe pesticides with low toxicity and organic fertilizer

ENTERPRISE

The on-site management of textile enterprises should be strengthened to reduce the energy consumption and emissions

LAUNDRY

The laundry habits of consumers should be op- timized and more municipal wastewater treatment plants should be built.

IMPROVEMENT: The government should encourage the cotton framers to adopt new advanced water-saving irrigation technique. And framer should use fertilizer and pesticides with low toxicity to reduce the damage to the land. Enterprise should strengthen the on-site management to reduce the consumption of material and energy. From the point of consumer, they should change their consumption habit by rejecting buying colored and single used onesie. Those measures can reduce the usage of dying and avoid the contamination of water.

IMPROVEMENT: By using advanced irrigation technique, the cost of material will improve temporarily, but the CO2 footprint in the cotton cultivation stage is reduced remarkably. Rejecting colored onesie avoid the usage of dye in the manufacturing stage, which reduce the containment of the water. In the beginning, the cost of a green version onesie is much higher than traditional onesie, but the difference will get smaller when the scope of production is getting bigger because updating the irrigation and manufacturing instrument is a one-step investment.
